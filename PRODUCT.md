# Product

## Register

brand+product

## Users

**Public site**: Potential B2B clients in La Réunion — gérants (owners/managers) of fuel stations and convenience stores, as well as purchasing managers at larger managed accounts. They arrive looking for a trustworthy distributor and a clear product offering.

**Ordering app**: A mixed audience ranging from independent gérants (likely mobile or tablet, varying digital literacy, placing regular restocking orders themselves) to professional procurement teams at managed accounts (desktop-first, higher volume, need catalog, order history, and invoicing). Both must feel fully served.

## Product Purpose

Inca Import is a B2B import distribution platform for beverages, snacks, and confectionery sold to fuel stations and convenience stores across La Réunion. The public-facing site establishes brand credibility and drives account sign-ups. The authenticated app lets existing clients browse the catalog, place restocking orders, and manage their account. Success means gérants trust us enough to make Inca their default supplier, and the ordering experience is fast enough that restocking feels effortless rather than administrative.

## Brand Personality

Efficient · Clear · Trustworthy. A premium B2B distributor with the confidence of a well-run operation — not a marketplace, not a FMCG promo machine. The feel: Linear meets Faire. Clean, generous, subtly animated, with typography doing real work. Professional without being cold. Approachable without being casual.

## Anti-references

- **Not generic Shopify/WooCommerce**: No templated e-commerce scaffolding. No default product grids, generic CTAs, or cookie-cutter layouts that read as "someone installed a theme."
- **Not loud or promotional**: No countdown timers, flash banners, %-off badge overload, or discount-driven visual hierarchy. This is a trusted partner relationship, not a flash sale.
- **Not luxury / inaccessible**: Not hyper-minimal or pretentiously sparse. The gérant checking stock on an Android phone at the counter needs to understand and use this without friction. Elegance is earned through clarity, not exclusion.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through clarity** — B2B buyers are staking their inventory on us. Prices, quantities, availability, and delivery terms must be immediately legible and never ambiguous. When in doubt, say more.
2. **Premium but grounded** — Reference-quality craft (subtle motion, considered spacing, strong type) without the coldness of luxury. The design should feel like it was made by people who understand the business, not a design agency showing off.
3. **Mobile-first, desktop-worthy** — The gérant on a phone at the counter is the primary ordering context. Design from mobile up; elevate gracefully for desktop. Never sacrifice touch ergonomics for visual elegance.
4. **Catalog is king** — The product catalog and ordering flow are the core value. Every page, section, and motion choice exists to get the buyer to the right product faster, not to showcase the brand.
5. **Motion signals quality, never novelty** — Subtle, purposeful animations (entrance, state change, feedback) elevate the experience above generic B2B tools without slowing the workflow. If an animation doesn't serve the buyer's task, cut it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance throughout. Mobile-first priority given the gérant use case: minimum 44px touch targets, generous tap areas, readable font sizes at arm's length. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations. Strong contrast for outdoor / mixed-lighting environments (fuel station counter). French language primary; consider right-to-left or multilingual needs if the catalog expands.
